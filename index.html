Guidelines for Labelling Agricultural Fields in the Amazon

1. Motivation

Our project seeks to improve the quality of cropland maps, which requires high-quality data to train and validate the models used to create the maps.

The Amazon biome has seen marked anthropogenic changes in the last five decades, catalyzed by expanding road networks, human migration, and economic integration with national and international markets. The overarching goal of this project is to support national efforts to monitor agricultural expansion in the Amazon's economic frontier. Specifically, we aim to fill important knowledge gaps in existing land cover data by mapping tree crop plantations that have economic value and substantial biodiversity impacts. To achieve this, we developed a probabilistic, satellite image-based reference dataset that distinguishes key tree crops (e.g., oil palm, cacao) from other land uses in cultivated regions and used this to train and assess tree crop mapping models.

The sampled points were loaded into Collect Earth Online (CEO) along with ancillary imagery to aid with data interpretation and create a labelling project. This project was carried out by three interpreters who visually analyzed the land cover type under each point using a key with examples of various tree crops and their imagery appearances. The CEO project incorporated SAR and optical data, Planet time-series imagery, high-resolution satellite basemaps, and GEDI height data as aids for interpretation. Participants included geography students and GIS professionals with varying familiarity with the study area.

2. Objective

  • Period of interpretation: Perennial crops from 2023 - 2024
  • Target agricultural fields: Oil Palm and Tree Crops
  • Where: Para, Brazil and Ucayali, Peru

3. The Interpreter Interface: Collect Earth Online

Collect Earth Online is a free, open-source system for viewing and interpreting high-resolution satellite imagery. It enables users to efficiently collect up-to-date information about their environment and observe changes over time.

The institutional page is named SERVIR_Amazonia_LCLUC, and the project is titled Test3TreeCropsValidationPara.

4. Rules

We have revised and simplified these rules on Dec 12, 2024.

This manual has been simplified for an interpretation campaign in CEO, focusing on the absence and presence of tree crops. If interpreters select "presence," the survey will display the following questions: Can you guess what type of tree crop? and Can you guess what stage of tree crop?
