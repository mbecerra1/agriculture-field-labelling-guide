Guidelines for Labelling Agricultural Fields in the Amazon

1. Motivation

Our project seeks to improve the quality of cropland maps, which requires high-quality data to train and validate the models used to create the maps.

The Amazon biome has seen marked anthropogenic changes in the last five decades, catalyzed by expanding road networks, human migration, and economic integration with national and international markets. The overarching goal of this project is to support national efforts to monitor agricultural expansion in the Amazon's economic frontier. Specifically, we aim to fill important knowledge gaps in existing land cover data by mapping tree crop plantations that have economic value and substantial biodiversity impacts. To achieve this, we developed a probabilistic, satellite image-based reference data collection dataset that distinguishes key tree crops (e.g. oil palm, cacao) from other land uses in cultivated regions, and used this to train and assess tree crop mapping models.

The sampled points were loaded into Collect Earth Online (CEO) along with ancillary imagery to aid with data interpretation to create a labelling project. This labelling project was undertaken by 3 separate interpreters, who visually interpreted the land cover type under each point following a key that provided examples of different tree crops and how to interpret their appearance in the imagery. The CEO project was configurated as base imagery the combination of SAR and optical data that highlight structural aspects that can be visually interpreted, time series of Planet imagery, high-resolution satellite base maps, with the GEDI-height data providing an additional aid to interpretation. Interpretation was undertaken by geography students and GIS professionals with different levels of familiarity with the study site.

2. Objective

  • Period of interpretation: Perennial crops from 2023 - 2024
  • Target agricultural fields: Oil Palm and Tree Crops
  • Where: Para, Brazil and Ucayali, Peru

3. The interpreter interface : Collect Earth Online

Collect Earth Online is a free, open-source system for viewing and interpreting high-resolution satellite imagery. It enables users to efficiently collect up-to-date information about their environment and observe changes over time.
