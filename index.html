Guidelines for Labelling Perennial Plantations in the Amazon

1. Motivation

  • Our project seeks to improve the quality of perennial crops maps, which requires high-quality data to train and validate the models used to create the maps.

  • The Amazon biome has seen marked anthropogenic changes in the last five decades, catalyzed by expanding road networks, human migration, and economic integration with national and international markets. The overarching goal of this project is to support national efforts to monitor agricultural expansion in the Amazon's economic frontier, we aim to fill important knowledge gaps in existing land cover data by mapping tree crop plantations: Cocoa and Oil Palm that have economic value and substantial biodiversity impacts.

  • The sampled points were loaded into Collect Earth Online (CEO) along with ancillary imagery to aid with data interpretation and create a labelling project. This project was carried out by three interpreters who visually analyzed the land cover type under each point using a key with examples of various tree crops and their imagery appearances. The CEO project incorporated SAR data (Sentinel 1) and optical data (Planet time-series imagery, high-resolution satellite basemaps such as Bing maps and Google Earth Pro) as aids for interpretation.

  • Participants included geography students and GIS professionals with varying familiarity with the study area.

    3. The Interpreter Interface: Collect Earth Online

    Collect Earth Online is a free, open-source system for viewing and interpreting high-resolution satellite imagery. It enables users to efficiently collect up-to-date information about their environment and observe changes over time.

    The institutional page is named SERVIR_Amazonia_LCLUC, and the project is titled Test3TreeCropsValidationPara.
