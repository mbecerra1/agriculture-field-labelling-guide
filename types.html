Guidelines for Labelling Agricultural Fields in the Amazon

A step-by-step approach for accurate mapping

1. Description

High-resolution satellite base maps allowed interpreters to interpretating different types of perennial agriculture. Following are examples of different perennial classes in the State of Para, Brazil. There is a list of perennial crops in State of Para: Cocoa, Citrus, and other perennial crop such as: woody pasture, silviculture, semmi-perennial crops.

2. Identification Criteria: Cocoa

  • Field Shape: Look for regular, geometric shapes (rectangular, square, and others) that looks like a tree cropland.
  • Vegetation Appearance in early stage of cocoa : Wider canopy, regular or irregular spacing, symmetrical or asymmetrical planting plots.
  • Vegetation Appearance in mature stage of cocoa : Apparent roughness, irregular spacing, aparent darker green color
  • Surrounding Context: Fields are often found near roads, households and infraestructure for seed drying. If is cocoa early stage must found near deforested areas

3. Identification Criteria: Citrus

  • Field Shape: Look for regular, geometric shapes (rectangular, square).
  • Vegetation Appearance: Narrow canopy, cropland typically shows uniform vegetation cover, regular spacing, greater spectral response of exposed soil, lower density of canopy, light green color.
  • Surrounding Context: Fields are often found near roads, and defined planting plots with road infrastructure.

4. Identification Criteria: Other perennial crop

  • Field Shape: Look for regular, geometric shapes (rectangular, square) that has visible tree crops.
  • Vegetation Appearance: Cropland typically shows uniform vegetation cover, visible tree crop platantion different from others above, apparent roughness, irregular spacing, aparent darker green color
  • Surrounding Context: Fields are often found somehow near to roads, and it is visible surrounded by yields

4. Identification Criteria: Oil Palm

  • Field Shape: Look for regular, geometric shapes (rectangular, square).
  • Vegetation Appearance: Cropland typically shows uniform vegetation cover, visible palm, surrounded by larger area of palms, darker greeness
  • Surrounding Context: Defined planting plots with road infrastructure.
